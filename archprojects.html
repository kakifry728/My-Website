MICU/CCU Remodel

Previous Next

Location: Seattle, WA
Type: Professional Design/Bid/Build Project
Date: 2017

The new MICU/CCU includes 18 beds arranged for maximum visibility and accessibility from 4 decentralized nurse station and 7 nurse alcoves with direct monitoring and supervision. The project implements the use of service walls, booms, patient lifting systems and concealed toilet units at each patient bedroom. All rooms are single occupancy including 2 Isolation rooms, 1 bariatric room and 1 bariatric/isolation patient bedroom. The service core includes equipment room and alcoves to keep corridors free of obstructions, a conference/break room, staff toilets, lockers and other smaller service rooms. All mechanical and electrical services were replaced.

I was responsible for construction administration on this project. Responsibilities included: managing sub-consultants, meeting with general contractor, conducting site visits and inspections, reviewing submittals, answering requests for information. The complexities of the systems included in this project resulted in a lot of hands on field investigations and working concurrently with the manufacturers, contractors, and the client.

Waterfalling

Previous Next

Location: Forks, WA
Type: School Design Studio
Date: Spring 2013

Located in Forks, WA, the Rainforest Art Center (RAC) is a hub for locals to gather and host cultural events and activities. This studio provided the opportunity for students in my class to work with the city planner as well as interview the residents of Forks. Ultimately, our designs were used to influence the professional architecture firm who would later design the new RAC.

My design drew inspiration from the abundant rainfall in the city of Forks. The program of the RAC consists of a commercial storefront with flexible multi-purpose areas at the rear on the ground floor. The second floor consists of an additional flexible space and a large open ballroom. The ballroom has an attached kitchen and restrooms. A glazed atrium in the center of the building provides a focal point and gathering area for users to watch as the water collected from the butterfly roofs are diffused through exterior rain chains.

Environmental Skylab

Previous Next

Location: Discovery Park, Seattle, WA
Type: School Design Studio
Date: Fall 2011

The task given was to design a field lab for high school students that will be used year-round for environmental study. The site is in Seattle’s Discovery Park at the edge between two contrasting ecotones: a meadow and a bluff. A series of platforms allows users to descend down the bluff and serves as an extension of the meadow. These lower platforms are where students are able to study the environment without interfering with the picturesque view from the meadow. The vertical central mass serves as the anchor that connects the planes to the core research and living spaces. Moving panels on the main research deck create protection from inclement weather while providing flexibility for students to survey different areas.

Folded

Previous Next

Location: Capitol Hill, Seattle, WA
Type: School Design Studio
Date: Spring 2012

This project included the design of a hybrid cooking school and farmers market located in Capitol Hill. Due to the very public location of the site, my initial response was to provide a building that is integrated into the fabric of the community and not just a school. In addition to public and private use, a third element became apparent after visiting and researching culinary schools: food flow. From these ideas I developed my concept further to reflect the interactions of the various elements’ flow throughout the space.

As exploring concept models, I saw that “food” is what brings everything together, it acts as the hinge between the public and private sectors. The circulation throughout the design has the public and private weaving through and around the vertical flow of food. The public markets allow users to pass through the building as they move along the busy corner of Broadway and Pine. The corner of the street is held by the student entrance to the school. As guests become food connoisseurs, students become chefs, and raw food becomes gourmet meals, the circulation of the different elements of the school culminate at the top in the public fine dining restaurant.

The Barn

Previous Next

Location: Woodinville, WA
Type: School Design Studio
Date: Fall 2012

For this project, my studio was given the task to design a restaurant and art gallery that celebrated art, wine, and food located near the entrance to the Chateau Ste. Michelle Winery in Woodinville. Art, wine, and food have grown and become more refined individually, however they are all related and when the three come together, it creates a sophisticated cultural experience. The fact that art and beauty can be found everywhere is physically represented in my design by having three smaller separate art galleries that are dispersed throughout the building, each intersecting the main spinal circulation path. Whether users have just entered, are in the bar, or are in the main dining area, through semi-opaque glass they see the art gallery that lies behind as a physical reminder of the beauty that surrounds them. The experience culminates in the private dining/wine tasting room where users are in an intimate setting celebrating their wine and food while being surrounded by art and the beauty of nature as they look out over the pond.
